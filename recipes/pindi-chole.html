Punjabi Pindi-Chole (Spicy)

Punjabi Pindi chole recipe

Experience the authentic flavors of Punjab with our Family Secret Pindi Chole Recipe!

This vegetarian delicacy showcases the perfect marriage of chickpeas and a medley of aromatic spices, creating a dish that is both hearty and satisfying. What sets Pindi Chole apart is its distinctive preparation, where chickpeas are cooked with a blend of robust spices such as coriander, dried pomegranate seeds, and garam masala

Must have ingredients list (5 servings)

  • 2 cup chickpeas
  • salt as required
  • 4 clove
  • 2 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 tablespoon coriander powder
  • 1 tablespoon dried pomegranate seeds
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper
  • 2 green chilli
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala powder
  • 4 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1 black cardamom
  • 2 small onion
  • 2 tablespoon ghee
  • 1 inch chopped ginger
  • water as required
  • 1 tablespoon dry mango powder
  • 2 cinnamon stick

    • For Garnishing

  • Coriander leaves

How to make Pindi-Chole

  1. Soak chickpeas overnight & chop the onion

    2. To begin with, add chickpeas to a bowl and add water to it. Soak the chickpeas overnight and drain the water. Now, chop the onion into thin slices.

  2. Prepare chole masala

    3. Then, keep a frying pan over medium flame , add pomegranate seeds and cumin to it. Dry roast them and transfer them into a mixer jar, grind into a fine powder.

  3. Pressure cook the chickpeas

    4. Keep a pressure cooker over medium flame and add enough water to it. Then add chickpeas, salt, cinnamon stick, cardamom and cloves. Cover the cooker with a lid and pressure cook for 15 to 20 minutes. Then turn the flame off and let the steam release itself.

  4. Coat the chickpeas with salt and spices

    5. From the prepared chickpea mixture, drain and separate the liquid and reserve it. To the remaining mixture add salt, pomegranate powder, mango powder, coriander, pepper and garam masala. Mix well and make sure the chickpeas are well coated with all the spices.

  5. Add ginger, green chillies & mix everything

    6. Top it up with ginger and green chillies. Mix again. Now, put a non-stick pan on medium heat and add ghee and vegetable oil to it. Pour this ghee mixture over the chickpea mixture and top it up with the retained liquid. Mix and combine well.

  6. Cook the Chana Pindi on medium flame

    7. Now put the pressure cooker with chickpea mixture over medium flame and cook until the moisture in the mixture dries out and the oil starts to separate from the other contents of the mixture. Once done, garnish your homemade Pindi Chole with chopped onion and serve hot!

Tips

  • You can add amchoor powder if you prefer tangy chole.
  • Garnish your pindi chole with freshly chopped coriander leaves.
  • You can also add lemon juice to this chole recipe.
